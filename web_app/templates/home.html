Cubes and Magic: The Gathering

A Brief History of Magic: The Gathering Cubes.

Magic: The Gathering cubes originated as a way for players to enjoy the drafting format without the ongoing cost of new booster packs. The concept likely took root in the early 2000s when enthusiasts of the game began curating collections of their favorite cards to draft from. Over time, these personalized sets evolved into carefully balanced assortments, enabling players to explore diverse strategies and interactions. Today, cubes range from collections of all-time favorites to theme-based selections, such as pauper, vintage, or modern legal cubes, each offering a unique drafting experience.

How Cubes are Drafted

Drafting a cube follows a similar structure to traditional Magic: The Gathering booster drafts, yet with a personal twist. Typically, each player begins with a "pack" of 15 cards randomly selected from the cube. Players pick one card from their pack to keep for their deck (P1P1), then pass the remaining cards to the player on their left. This process continues until all cards are drafted. Since the cube is a curated collection of cards, each draft round is rich with potent synergies and strategic depth, making every choice crucial and every draft unique.
